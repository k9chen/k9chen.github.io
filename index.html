I am a third-year undergraduate at UVA majoring in neuroscience and chemistry with a minor in bioethics. I am respectively advised by Drs. Petr Tvrdik and Richard Dortch in the Departments of Neurosurgery and Translational Neuroscience at UVA School of Medicine and Barrow Neurological Institute, where I conduct neurologic disease-oriented preclinical research.

My main research focuses on translating pathophysiological biomarkers and mechanisms of neurovascular diseases into novel diagnostic and therapeutic tools for clinical applications via the integration of computational and experimental methods.

Work in progress...
Here are some current research projects that I am working on:

1. Mapping phenotypic and spatial heterogeneity of myeloid cell subtypes in the recombinase-mediated RC::RLTG reporter mouse following transient middle cerebral artery occlusion.

Collaborator(s) @ UVA School of Medicine: Khadijeh Sharifi, Petr Tvrdik

2. Developing a Rician distributed multi-compartmental DWI-MRI signal simulation via Monte Carlo to assay pathologically realistic tissue microstructure in trauma-induced peripheral nerves.

Collaborator(s) @ Barrow Neurological Institute: Thammathida Ketsiri, Richard Dortch

3. Training U-Net-based AI/ML models for biomedical image segmentation of spinal pathology.

Collaborator(s) @ Brown University: Daniel Zhu
